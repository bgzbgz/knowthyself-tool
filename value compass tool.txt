VALUES COMPASS Tool  
Tool explanation and guide 
[01/08]
TOOL overview

Align your personal values with your professional actions to create a fulfilling and purpose-driven career. This tool will help you navigate your decisions with confidence, knowing they are rooted in what truly matters to you.
Type of Tool
Individual
Suggested preparation time
30 minutes
Logical fit 
This tool is essential for ensuring that your actions and decisions are aligned with your core values, which is vital for long-term success and satisfaction in both your personal and professional life.
[02/08]
TOOL PREVIEW

[03/08]
Purpose
By using this tool, you’ll gain clarity on your core values and ensure that they guide your daily decisions. This alignment is crucial for building a career that is not only successful but also deeply fulfilling.

[04/08]
Step-by-Step Instructions
1. Identify Your Core Values
Reflect on the values that are most important to you. These are the principles that guide your behavior and decisions in both personal and professional settings.
Example of correct entry
"Integrity, creativity, and continuous learning."
Example of a common pitfall
Choosing values that are trendy or expected rather than those that genuinely resonate with you.
2. Evaluate аlignment
Assess how well your current actions and decisions align with your identified values. This will help you identify areas where you may need to make adjustments.
Example of correct entry
"Aligning my role as a leader with the value of integrity by fostering transparency and accountability within my team."
Example of a common pitfall
Ignoring discrepancies between your values and actions, leading to internal conflict and dissatisfaction.
[05/08]

mistakes to avoid
1. Superficial choices
Ensure that your values are deeply personal and not just selected based on external expectations. 
2. Lack of action
It’s crucial to take steps to align your actions with your values, rather than simply identifying them. 
[06/08]


f.a.q. and answers

1. What if I discover a misalignment between my values and actions?
Use this as an opportunity to adjust your behavior or seek changes in your environment that allow for better alignment.
2. How often should I revisit my values?
Regularly, especially when facing major decisions or changes in your life or career.
[07/08]
The Science and Logic Behind the Tool

This tool is based on the understanding that values-driven decisions lead to greater satisfaction and success. When your actions align with your core values, you experience less internal conflict and more fulfillment in your career.


[08/08]
Final Tips
Consistency
Ensure your values consistently guide your decisions and actions.
Clarity
Be clear about how your values influence your professional path.
Engagement
Use this tool to stay connected to what matters most, driving both personal and professional growth.
